\noin
Lecture notes toward the quantum field theory textbook of $\sim$2046: the book 
that will exist once the developments of 2004--2026 --- generalized and 
non-invertible symmetries, the bootstrap renaissance, monotonicity theorems, the 
algebraic/entanglement revival, the lattice--continuum program --- are digested 
into pedagogy the way Wilson's revolution was digested into the textbooks of the 
1990s. The notes open with an annotated table of contents (twelve parts,
fifty-eight chapters): each chapter comes with a paragraph explaining why it
exists and pointers into the current literature (Seiberg, Witten, Shao, 
C\'ordova, Sch\"afer-Nameki, Rychkov, Simmons-Duffin, Komargodski, Gaiotto, 
Rastelli, Yin, Polyakov's heirs all). Chapters are then being written one at a 
time, starting with Part I. The cited papers are collected in \texttt{assets/}, 
indexed by arXiv number; see \texttt{assets/README.md}.\\
